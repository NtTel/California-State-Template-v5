Alert Banner

The alert banner is for important, sometimes time-sensitive information. Reserve alerts for information the audience must read before anything else. It goes above the site navigation on any page.

The alert banner is a band that extends across the width of the page.

It includes:

  • An optional icon, selected from a list of font icon options
  • A text field, which includes the ability to link to other pages
  • An “X” close icon that allows people to dismiss the alert

Nunjucks include
Add the alert key with the path to your alert html file alert: "../modules/alert-banner.html" after your eleventy layout key declaration in the front matter data on your page. 
---
title: Alert Banner
layout: page.njk
alert: "../modules/alert-banner.html"
---

Or include alert banner module into your page directly after skip-to-content component:

 <header role="banner" id="header" class="global-header">

Usage

When and how to use it

Use alert banners sparingly. They alarm people and distract from other content.

Be concise with your alert banner content. Use a link to send people to a page with the details.

Only use one alert banner on a page. Make sure the alert is relevant to the page it is on. Only duplicate the alert on other pages if it applies to those pages.

Use alert banners to tell people about:

  • Open application periods (informational)
  • Planned maintenance (informational)
  • Approaching deadlines (warning)
  • Services that are down (danger)
  • Positive feedback (success)

Choose an icon that supports the tone of your alert.

How not to use it

Do not put an alert banner on every page on your site. If people see alerts too often, they will start to ignore them and not read the content.

Do not put more than one alert banner on a page.

Do not use alert banners to simply highlight important content. They are for information that is new, relevant, and temporary.
